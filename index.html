rIoT: Cybersecurity in the Internet of Things

Kim Fong, Kurt Hepler, Rohit Raghavan, Peter Rowland

Ineffective security in IoT devices led to one of the largest DDoS attacks in history. Yet, federal regulators have had difficulty showing that insecure devices cause a sufficiently “substantial injury” to warrant regulatory enforcement under consumer protection law. Our final project will contribute to the literature on IoT security by trying to quantify the harms caused by insecure Internet-connected devices. We will bring together, technical, legal, and economic analysis to shed light on the true costs of IoT security. What consumer harms do insecure devices cause, and who should bear the burden of preventing or mitigating cyber attacks?
